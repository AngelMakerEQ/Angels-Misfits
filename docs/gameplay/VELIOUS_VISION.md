# Angels Misfits - Velious Vision

## Purpose

This document defines the gameplay philosophy and design goals for Angels Misfits.

Angels Misfits aims to preserve the feeling and identity of classic EverQuest, with Velious serving as the primary design reference point.

The goal is not to recreate every historical limitation of EverQuest. The goal is to preserve the elements that created the classic EverQuest experience while allowing practical improvements that support long-term enjoyment, administration, and maintenance.

---

# Core Philosophy

Angels Misfits should feel like a classic EverQuest world.

Players should experience:

- A dangerous and immersive world
- Meaningful exploration
- Distinct zones with unique identities
- Strong class identities
- Rewarding progression
- Memorable NPCs and encounters
- Valuable equipment upgrades
- A sense of adventure and discovery

Historical accuracy is prioritized where it directly affects the player experience.

Historical limitations that primarily created inconvenience without improving gameplay may be adjusted.

---

# What Should Be Preserved

The following areas should strongly prioritize historical authenticity.

## World Design

Preserve:

- Zone identity
- Zone atmosphere
- Geographic relationships
- Classic layouts where practical
- Exploration-driven gameplay

Changes should avoid making the world feel like a modern theme park.

---

## Progression

Preserve:

- Meaningful character advancement
- Gradual power growth
- Importance of equipment upgrades
- Distinct progression paths
- The feeling of earning accomplishments

Progression should remain rewarding even when quality-of-life improvements are introduced.

---

## Encounters

Preserve:

- Dangerous environments
- Strategic combat
- Importance of preparation
- Distinct NPC behaviors
- Meaningful group roles

Encounters should avoid becoming trivial unless intentionally redesigned.

---

## Classes

Preserve:

- Class identity
- Unique strengths and weaknesses
- Meaningful abilities
- Distinct gameplay roles

Quality-of-life improvements should not remove the reasons players choose different classes.

---

## Itemization

Preserve:

- Valuable upgrades
- Interesting item choices
- Meaningful loot progression
- Recognition of rare items

Items should maintain a sense of accomplishment.

---

# Acceptable Modern Improvements

Angels Misfits is a private server intended for long-term personal enjoyment and maintenance.

Quality-of-life improvements are acceptable when they improve the experience without damaging the core EverQuest identity.

Examples may include:

- Reduced unnecessary downtime
- Improved administration tools
- Increased solo viability
- Improved progression pacing for a solo player environment
- Reduced unnecessary penalties
- Better documentation and management workflows
- Technical improvements that do not alter the gameplay identity

Each improvement should be evaluated based on whether it preserves the intended player experience.

---

# Solo Player Considerations

Angels Misfits is designed primarily as a private solo-play environment.

The server does not need to follow the same balance assumptions as a public multiplayer server.

Changes may be considered to support solo progression, including:

- Adjusted experience rates
- Reduced or removed experience loss penalties
- Improved accessibility to content normally requiring groups
- Adjusted encounter difficulty
- Custom quality-of-life systems

These changes should preserve the feeling of EverQuest progression rather than simply accelerate character power.

---

# Authenticity vs Convenience

When authenticity and convenience conflict, evaluate the impact on the player's experience.

Prioritize authenticity when the change affects:

- World immersion
- Exploration
- Progression identity
- Class identity
- Encounters
- Item value
- Player accomplishments

Prioritize convenience when the historical limitation primarily creates:

- Excessive downtime
- Administrative burden
- Technical difficulty
- Frustration without meaningful gameplay value

When uncertain, document the tradeoff and choose the option that best supports the long-term goals of Angels Misfits.

---

# Visual Identity

The intended visual experience should reflect classic EverQuest presentation while maintaining technical compatibility.

Priorities include:

- Classic zone atmosphere
- Classic environmental presentation
- Classic loading screen presentation
- Reduced modern visual elements
- Appropriate NPC appearance

Player choice should remain available where practical.

Examples:

- Luclin player models may remain available as an optional player choice.
- NPCs should prioritize classic-era visual presentation unless intentionally changed.

---

# Future Expansion Philosophy

Velious is the primary design reference point, not necessarily a permanent expansion restriction.

If future development considers later expansions such as Luclin or Planes of Power, decisions should be evaluated based on whether they support the overall Angels Misfits experience.

Future content should be judged by:

- Does it preserve the feeling of EverQuest?
- Does it improve the player experience?
- Does it maintain world consistency?
- Does it create meaningful progression?
- Does it introduce unnecessary complexity?

Expansion beyond Velious should be intentional rather than automatic.

---

# Decision Rule

When considering a gameplay change:

Ask:

1. Does this preserve the feeling of classic EverQuest?
2. Does this improve the long-term experience of the server?
3. Does this maintain meaningful progression and identity?
4. Does this introduce unnecessary complexity?
5. Is the change documented?

The goal of Angels Misfits is not to recreate the past perfectly.

The goal is to preserve what made classic EverQuest memorable while creating a sustainable version of that experience.
